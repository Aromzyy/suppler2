Skin Conditions and Their Characteristics

Eczema

Characterized by varying colors including pink, red, brown, purple, or gray, often with itchy, greasy, or oily scaly patches.

Psoriasis

Causes scaly, silvery, sharply defined skin plaques, possibly itchy or asymptomatic, commonly on the scalp, elbows, knees, and lower back.

Cellulitis

A medical emergency when symptoms appear, characterized by painful, swollen skin that may ooze and spread quickly.

Measles

Includes fever, sore throat, and a red rash spreading from the face down the body, which might be more difficult to detect on darker skin.

Basal Cell Carcinoma

Can cause shiny and pearly areas with a potential for bleeding or oozing wounds that struggle to heal.

Squamous Cell Carcinoma

Often occurs in areas exposed to UV radiation, characterized by a scaly reddish patch of skin which may progress to a raised bump.

Melanoma

The most serious form of skin cancer, appearing as moles with irregular shapes and colors, often in less sun-exposed areas in people of color.

Lupus

May cause a butterfly-shaped rash on the cheeks, as well as other skin and systemic symptoms, with an increased chance of PIH and scarring in people of color.
